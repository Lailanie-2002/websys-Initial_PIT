Furniture has evolved over millennia, starting with ancient civilizations using natural materials like wood and stone. From the simplicity of Greek and Roman designs to the ornate Gothic period, furniture mirrored the architectural styles of each era. The Renaissance brought elegance and proportion, while the Baroque era embraced opulence and elaborate ornamentation. The Industrial Revolution made furniture more accessible, leading to styles like Victorian and Arts and Crafts. The 20th century saw innovative movements like Bauhaus and mid-century modernism. Today, contemporary furniture focuses on sustainability, minimalism, and technological integration, shaping the ever-changing landscape of design.


Furniture design has always been an important part of architecture. It is defined as the movable items inside space and each one of them plays a specific role in people’s ordinary activities. This role can be to sit, eat, store clothes, decorate, light the room, etc. Thus, chairs, tables, closets, and lampshades are all examples of what we call furniture, and it is hard to imagine humans' daily lives without it. Its first appearance dates back to the Neolithic period, as evidence suggests.
